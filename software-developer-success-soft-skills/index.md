# Software Developer Success - Soft Skills & Testing

## Introduction

### Ramping Up in a New Role

- Ask questions
- Pair programming or shadow experienced coworkers

### Tribal Knowledge to Learn from Others

- Which parts are good and bad in the codebase
- Which are the patterns one should follow and which ones should be avoided
- What's expected for testing & maintainability
- What's the PR process like ? How many reviewers, what kind of feedback to expect

- What's the pipeline to deployment like ? (how many stages is it going through before it's deployed)
- Which documentation sources are reliable (are they kept up to date)

## Developer Growth Essentials

### Time Estimation

#### Common obstacles when executing tasks

- Under-communicating
- Not understainding how long a task will take
- Not having enough information about the task

#### Estimation

- Practice breaking tasks down
- Guess how long it will take to complete the task and compare it to the actual time it took
- Understand how long it takes to get to completion in your company process
- Ex:
  - Code Complete
    - PR Review
    - 1-14 days ?
      - Test => Stage => Prod Env
      - 1-7 days ?
        - Deployment
        - 1-7 days ?

#### Over Communicate

**What**

- What is **done**
- What is **in-progress**
- What are **blockers**
- What is at **risk of not being delivered**

**To Who**

- To your **manager**
- To your **teammates** working on this task
- To your **stakeholders** relying on this work

#### Gather Information

Gathering more information about the task provides flexibility when obstacles are hit

- What is the **task**
  - Most tickets are very detailed, so it's up to you to understand what's it's trying to ask for, what are the edge conditions that you want to handle
- What are the **validation steps**
  - to determine that this task is correct and done
- Who are the **stakeholders**
- Who is **relying** on the task
  - A lot of work doesn't happen in isolation. Usually, there is a pipeline and other people are probably relying on this work
- Why does this work **matter**
  - What's the importance to your team, to the people relying on it, to the business
  - When is it **needed**

### Self Assessment & Career Growth

#### Feedback Loops

To ensure you're still growing, it is important to create feedback loops from those around you

- You want to be aiming for weekly or bi-weekly feedback, so that you can address it early and often preventing these issues from snowballing into real big problems

Ask very specific questions
Ex :

- Is there something I could have done for this task better ?
- What areas do you think I could improve upon ?

When trying to go for promotions and stuff :

- Do you have any suggestions for how I can take on more responsibility or expand my role ?

To see what they expect from you :

- What goals or objectives should I focus on in the upcoming months ?

To know how your manager is keeping track of your progress :

- How do I gain visibility into my work ?

#### Asking for Help

Timeboxing : go into an agreement with your manager about how long is it okay for you to be stuck on a problem ?

When asking for help :

- Restating what you think the problem was and what you're supposed to do next. If you had made an incorrect assumption, they can immediately figure out and tell you, and then steer you back onto the right course

Don't forget to express gratitude after the help

#### Surfacing Your Accomplishments

Why surface your accomplishments ?

- Relying on others noticing your work is risky because people are busy, so your contributions might go unnoticed
- Silence can easily be mistaken for inactivity, which is not a narrative you want to be associated with
- Promotions is still a subjective decision made by people and that decision depends on trust
- If they're unaware of your impact and accomplishments, they cannot confidently entrust you with more chanllenges and responsibility
- Owning your narrative is not about self-promotion or arrogance, it's about creating clarity and trust

How to surface your accomplishments :

- Create & regularly update a brag doc (a document that lists your accomplishments), a metric of your impact is even better
- Create regular communication loops with your manager
- If your receive positive feedback, ask them to put it in writing, take a screenshot, and put it in your brag doc to send to your manager
- You want to be continuously sending that kind of information back to your manager
- Understand the promotion and rewards process, before you actually want that promotion (A year or a year and a half beforehand)
- And then, checking with your manager whether you're on track, so that you're not gonna be as surprised about whether you're gonna get that promotion or not

### Preparation Takeaways

- **Ramp up and Capture Knowledge Quickly** : Start delivering efficiently by understanding the codebase, company processes, and norms through shadowing and pair programming.
- **Focus on Maintainable and Testable Code** : Write quality code that is easy to maintain and change. Use tests to understand systems and work safely, embracing test-driven development even if it's undervalued in your company.
- **Overcome Common Blockers** : Have a clear debugging process, gather information, improve estimation skills, and over-communicate to execute tasks effectively.
- **Seek Help and Prepare for Growth** : Be proactive in asking for help and understand the steps to position yourself for promotions.
