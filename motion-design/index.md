# Motion in UX Design

## Why is Motion Important ?

> Motion tells stories, Everything in an app is a sequence, and motion is your guide. For every button clicked and screen transition, there is a story that follows - Graig Dehner

- Motion serves as an explanation to the use of how one gets to and from various places in the interface
- Motion plays a large role in how users perceive what objects in the interface are and what they do
  - **Skeuomorphic behavior** - The practice of making digital interfaces or objects resemble their real-world counterparts in appearance or functionality.
    - It helps users transition from physical tools to digital ones by leveraging their familiarity with the real world
    - _Skeuomorphic design_ was popular in earlier interfaces (e.g., iOS 6). Today, design trends lean towards **flat** or **minimalist** design, but skeuomorphic elements still appear in subtle ways to enhance usability.
  - **rules** - Motion plays a significant role in defining and reinforcing new rules
  - **interactional and visual cues** - It implies that there is some sort of cause and effect in the interface, motion comes after the cue and either aligns or conflicts with these cues

Summary - Motion has the potential to connect with user mental models, and drive product results

## What is a Microinteraction ?

Microinteraction are _contained product moments_ that are meant for a single use case

- Microinteraction make the user experience smooth and easy

## What is considered a Microinteraction ?

- scrollbar
- alarms
- buttons
- pull to refresh
- swiping
- notifications
- etc...

## How Microinteractions impact user experiences

- They are small details that significantly impact on the impression the product has on its users
- In most cases the difference between products we tolerate and products we love are the quality of the microinteractions
- They provide feedback and keep user informed
- They can create seamless experiences
- They encourage engagement
